## 1. Section positioning (HKFP's own words)
> "Critical commentary and analysis on Hong Kong, China and Taiwan affairs from HKFP's community of columnists and expert voices."

It is an **op-ed / analysis** column, not news reporting. Each piece carries a footer disclaimer:
> "Advocates for ideas and draws conclusions based on the interpretation of facts and data."
i.e. opinion is allowed, but it must be **evidence-based**.

## 3. Topic mix (recent ~6 months)
Recurring themes, in rough order of frequency:
1. **Governance & rule of law** — LegCo's role, national security rules, court reasoning, public consultation processes.
2. **Climate / sustainability / energy** — Five-Year Plan, data-centre power use, oil prices, climate × culture.
3. **Vulnerable groups & social policy** — migrant domestic workers, children's rights vs Big Tech, animal welfare, obesity campaign.

## 4. Format & house style (from 5 full-text samples)

| Metric | Range observed |
|---|---|
| Length | **~1,000–1,400 words** (mean ≈ 1,170) |
| Paragraphs | 17–33 short paragraphs |
| Sub-headings (H2/H3) | **None** — flowing prose, no listicles |
| Images | 1 hero image + a small number of inline photos with credits |
| Bylines | "By <Name>" at top; author bio block at bottom |
| Tone | First-person allowed, analytical, often opens with a concrete anecdote / news hook, ends with a clear policy ask or rhetorical close |
| Sourcing | Inline hyperlinks to court rulings, government press releases, NGO reports, academic studies |

### Typical structure
1. **Hook** — a recent news event or vivid concrete scene (e.g. "One of the changes I have noticed since our last District Council was replaced by a patriots-only gathering…").
2. **Why it matters** — situate the issue in HK governance / society.
3. **Evidence** — data, statute text, comparison with other jurisdictions (mainland China, US, EU often cited).
4. **Counter-argument acknowledged** — show the official line, then critique it.
5. **Policy ask / takeaway** — what should change, who should act.

### Footer (always)
"Make a donation to our newsroom of any sum…" — boilerplate, not part of the article.

## 5. What this means for our pitch this afternoon

**Ask the editor:**
1. Are student-authored pieces eligible under "Guest Contributor", or do they require a faculty co-byline?
2. Word-count ceiling — confirm ~1,200 words is the sweet spot.
3. Lead time from submission to publication, and whether they want exclusivity.
4. Editing process — do they sub-edit heavily, or is the writer expected to deliver near-publishable copy?
5. Sourcing requirements — comfortable level of inline links; any required disclosures (e.g. that work was produced in a HKBU course).
6. Topic appetite — would they take a series across the semester, or prefer one-off strong pieces?

**What we can offer (to make it easy to say yes):**
- A pre-screened pipeline of 1,000–1,300 word evidence-based pieces, each built on a structured data-governance project (Spring 2026 topics listed above).
- Built-in fact-checking: every claim already mapped to a public dataset or government source as part of the course rubric.
- Faculty editorial pass before submission, so the editor receives near-final copy.
- Optional original data visualisations / charts the section currently lacks (most pieces are text-only).
